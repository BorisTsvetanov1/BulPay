# Usability Research - Hypotheses & Validation Questions

---

## 📐 Hypotheses

### H1 - Core Flow Simplicity

In a first-time use test (n=6), at least **5/6 participants** will complete *"Send €15 to an already saved recipient"* — beginning from the login screen and ending at the *"Successful transfer"* screen — **without moderator assistance in 3 minutes or less**.

---

### H2 - Home Screen UI Clarity

At least **5/6 participants** will tap the correct entry point to start a transfer from the Home screen **within 15 seconds**, without exploring more than 1 unrelated feature *(e.g. tapping on "Add card" or "Help/Settings")*.

---

### H3 - Trustworthiness

After finishing the transfer, at least **5/6 participants** will:

- Rate their confidence that the money was sent at **≥ 5/7**, and
- Identify **at least two** confirmation cues from the UI *(e.g. "Successful transfer" message, green tick sign, receipt details, transaction reference)*.

---

### H4 - Security

After completing the transfer, at least **5/6 participants** will:

- Rate their feeling of safety when sending €15 as **5 or higher** on a 7-point scale, and
- Fewer than **2 participants** will question or resist the additional PIN confirmation step.

---

## 🎬 Test Scenario

> *"Imagine you are on your lunch break at work. Your daughter Andrea calls you urgently asking you to send her €15 so she can purchase new study books for school."*

---

## ❓ Post-Test Questions

1. On a scale from **1 to 7**, how confident are you that the money got sent? What on the screen made you feel that the transfer was successful?
2. On a scale from **1 to 7**, how safe did you feel sending this money? How did the second PIN confirmation step feel to you?
3. Compared to the payment apps you normally use, did this feel **simpler, similar, or more complex**? Why?
4. Where did you feel **unsure or slow down**, and what made you hesitate?
5. Was there any step or information that felt **unnecessary**?
6. Let's go back to the home screen. Was there anything on this screen that you expected to **work differently**?
7. Is there anything else that we haven't discussed, that you would like to add?
